    <h1>Technical Diving</h1>
    <p style="font-size: 1.2rem; max-width: 800px; margin-bottom: 3rem;">Technical diving is about extending your depth, duration, and safety envelopes by adopting redundancy, strict dive planning, and advanced gas mixtures. As a TDI Technical Instructor, I provide training that emphasizes safety margins, gas management, and critical thinking.</p>

    <div class="courses-list grid-2-cards" style="gap: 3rem;">
      <!-- Course 1 -->
      <div class="card" style="border-color: rgba(255, 82, 82, 0.3);" id="course-adv-nitrox">
        <span class="card-meta" style="color: #ff5252;">TDI Entry Tech</span>
        <h3 style="font-size: 1.8rem; margin-bottom: 1rem;">TDI Advanced Nitrox</h3>
        <p>This course is the foundation of technical diving. It qualifies you to use enriched air nitrox mixtures from 21% up to 100% oxygen for breathing and decompression purposes, significantly shortening decompression times.</p>
        <div style="display: grid; grid-template-columns: repeat(auto-fit, minmax(200px, 1fr)); gap: 1.5rem; margin: 1.5rem 0; padding: 1rem; background: rgba(2, 6, 23, 0.4); border-radius: 8px;">
          <div><strong>Prerequisites:</strong> SDI Advanced Adventure (or equivalent) & Nitrox Diver</div>
          <div><strong>Duration:</strong> 2-3 Days (4 dives)</div>
          <div><strong>Max Depth:</strong> 40 meters</div>
          <div><strong>Focus:</strong> Gas analysis, oxygen tracking, cylinder rigging</div>
        </div>
        <h4 style="color: var(--color-primary); margin-top: 1rem; margin-bottom: 0.5rem;">Core Skills Mastered:</h4>
        <p>You will learn to execute proper gas switches underwater, analyze and label custom nitrox mixes, calculate oxygen toxicity loads (CNS/OTU), and plan gas requirements for technical profiles.</p>
      </div><!-- Course 2 -->
      <div class="card" style="border-color: rgba(255, 82, 82, 0.5);" id="course-deco-procedures">
        <span class="card-meta" style="color: #ff5252;">TDI Core Tech</span>
        <h3 style="font-size: 1.8rem; margin-bottom: 1rem;">TDI Decompression Procedures</h3>
        <p>Move beyond standard recreational no-decompression limits. This course teaches you to plan and execute staged decompression dives down to a maximum of 45 meters using nitrox or oxygen mixtures.</p>
        <div style="display: grid; grid-template-columns: repeat(auto-fit, minmax(200px, 1fr)); gap: 1.5rem; margin: 1.5rem 0; padding: 1rem; background: rgba(2, 6, 23, 0.4); border-radius: 8px;">
          <div><strong>Prerequisites:</strong> Advanced Nitrox, 25 logged dives, Min. age 18</div>
          <div><strong>Duration:</strong> 3-4 Days (4 open water dives)</div>
          <div><strong>Max Depth:</strong> 45 meters</div>
          <div><strong>Focus:</strong> Ascent profiles, gas margins, fail-safe procedures</div>
        </div>
        <h4 style="color: var(--color-primary); margin-top: 1rem; margin-bottom: 0.5rem;">Why this course is essential:</h4>
        <p>Often combined with Advanced Nitrox, Deco Procedures teaches you to manage complex equipment (twinsets or sidemount with deco cylinders) and respond to failures (gas losses, regulator freezes, lost deco gas) when a direct ascent to the surface is not an option.</p>
      </div><!-- Course 3 -->
      <div class="card" style="border-color: rgba(255, 82, 82, 0.3);" id="course-intro-to-tech">
        <span class="card-meta" style="color: #ff5252;">TDI Entry Tech</span>
        <h3 style="font-size: 1.8rem; margin-bottom: 1rem;">TDI Intro to Tech</h3>
        <p>Introduce yourself to the world of technical diving. This course is designed to transition recreational divers into technical configurations, <strong>offered exclusively in Sidemount configuration</strong> for streamlined flexibility and redundancy without committing to deep or decompression diving immediately.</p>
        <div style="display: grid; grid-template-columns: repeat(auto-fit, minmax(200px, 1fr)); gap: 1.5rem; margin: 1.5rem 0; padding: 1rem; background: rgba(2, 6, 23, 0.4); border-radius: 8px;">
          <div><strong>Prerequisites:</strong> SDI Open Water Diver (or equiv.), Min. age 15, 25 logged dives</div>
          <div><strong>Duration:</strong> 2-3 Days (min. 3 dives)</div>
          <div><strong>Max Depth:</strong> Limits of current certification (recreational)</div>
          <div><strong>Configuration:</strong> Sidemount ONLY</div>
        </div>
        <h4 style="color: var(--color-primary); margin-top: 1rem; margin-bottom: 0.5rem;">What you will learn:</h4>
        <p>Technical diving concepts, dive planning, buoyancy control, horizontal trim, advanced propulsion techniques (frog and back kicks), sidemount valve management, regulator switches, and standard technical team communication.</p>
      </div><!-- Course 4 -->
      <div class="card" id="course-sidemount">
        <span class="card-meta" style="color: var(--color-secondary);">Equipment Specialty</span>
        <h3 style="font-size: 1.8rem; margin-bottom: 1rem;">TDI Sidemount Diver</h3>
        <p>Moving your tanks from your back to your sides offers unparalleled flexibility, redundancy, and streamlined movement. Popular for overhead environments (caves/wrecks) and increasingly favored by cold-water lake divers for safety and back-comfort.</p>
        <div style="display: grid; grid-template-columns: repeat(auto-fit, minmax(200px, 1fr)); gap: 1.5rem; margin: 1.5rem 0; padding: 1rem; background: rgba(2, 6, 23, 0.4); border-radius: 8px;">
          <div><strong>Prerequisites:</strong> Open Water Diver, Min. age 18</div>
          <div><strong>Duration:</strong> 2-3 Days (4 lake dives)</div>
          <div><strong>Configuration:</strong> Two independent cylinders mounted on harness sides</div>
          <div><strong>Focus:</strong> Hose routing, cylinder trim, gas switching</div>
        </div>
        <h4 style="color: var(--color-primary); margin-top: 1rem; margin-bottom: 0.5rem;">What you will learn:</h4>
        <p>Proper adjustment of sidemount harnesses, positioning cylinders to maintain a flat horizontal trim, valve management, regulator switches, and handling independent cylinders in and out of the water.</p>
      </div>
